Pseudocoding an everyday task: Brushing my teeth in dorms

Grab my toothbrush, cup, and toothpaste in my room, and also grab my student ID card so I can get back in
Exit my dorm and head to the bathroom
If there is water on the counter, grab a paper towel
    Try to wipe it down. If it is not dry, grab another paper towel and repeat. Expect to use roughly six to eight paper towels on this.
Grab two paper towels and place them on the counter on top of each other
Place my toothbrush, cup, toothpaste, and card on the paper towels
Open the toothpaste tube and apply some toothpaste to the toothbrush. Close the toothpaste tube.
Grab another paper tower to be used to grab the faucet handle when needed.
Brush my teeth.
Brush my tongue.
Turn on the faucet using the designated paper towel.
Clean the toothpaste off the toothbrush.
Fill the cup.
Brush my tongue again with the rinsed toothbrush.
Clean the toothbrush again.
Turn off the faucet using the designated paper towel.
Rinse my mouth with the water in the cup.
Spit the water out.
Turn on the faucet using the designated paper towel.
Grab soap.
Wash my hands.
Rinse out the cup.
Turn off the faucet using the designated paper towel.
Wipe my hands off with more paper towels.
Grab the toothbrush, toothpaste, cup, and card with one hand.
Grab the three paper towels in use with my other hand.
Toss the paper towels out.
Grab one more paper towel to be used to touch the door handle to my room.
Exit the bathroom and head for my room.
Unlock the door using the ID card, making sure not to touch the door handle. Use the paper towel to punch in the code and open the door.
Once in the room, toss out the paper towel.
Put away the toothbrush, toothpaste, cup, and card.
Contemplate the safety of having a bathroom that is only cleaned once a week shared between an entire floor during the COVID-19 pandemic.
